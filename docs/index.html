FIVE LARGE PROTESTS IN U.S HISTORY


A moment from the George Floyd protests that happened nationwide

The Black Lives Matter movement is the newest campaign in direct opposition to police brutality that originated in 2013. After the acquittal of George Zimmerman in the murder Trayvon Martin. In the past protesters’ main rally chant was “No Justice, No Peace”, but today’s activists are taking it a step further by demanding state officials to reduce the funding of state law enforcement.

Here are the country’s five of the top protests in terms of attendance:

  • Nationwide in 2020 with an estimated 26 million protestors.
  • Women’s March in 2017 drew about 5.6 million people nationwide.
  • March for Our Lives , a student led protest in 2018 against gun violence, drew about 2 million people.
  • Women’s March in 2018 drew about 1.5 million protestors.
  • March on Washington for Lesbian, Gay and Bi Equal Rights and Liberation drew about 1 million people in 1993.
